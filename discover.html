Monterrey, México

Monterrey is the capital and most populated city in the mexican state of Nuevo León and the third largest city in Mexico just behind Guadalajara and Mexico City(CDMX). The metropolitan area of Monterrey is the second most productive and industrialized in Mexico with a GDP of US$140 billion in 2015, it is also the second largest metropolitan area in the country of Mexico with an estimated population of 5,341,171 people in 2020. Monterrey is one of the most livable cities in Mexico, and acoording to experts in the field, San Pedro Garza Garcia which is part of the metropolitan area of Monterrey, is the city with the best quality of life in Mexico and the richest in Latin america. It has luxury shopping malls, large green areas, as well as important colleges, hospitals and parks.

Cerro de la silla

The Cerro de la Silla is a mountain and natural monument located in the city of Monterrey, Nuevo León. Named for its distinctive saddle-shaped profile when viewed from the west, it is a well-known symbol of the city of Monterrey, despite being located half in the city of Guadalupe and the other half in Monterrey. It was set aside as a natural monument by the Mexican government in 1991, the mountain, or hill, as mountanins are often referred to in Mexico, is a popular recreational area and is often climbed by hikers who take a 5.3km trail to reach the top. The ascent is considered to be fairly difficult, taking approximately 3 hours to complete. A panoramic view of the city of Monterrey can be seen from the top.

📍 Location

The city of Monterrey is located in the Northeast part of the country of Mexico and it is the capital of the state of Nuevo León, this state limits with the southern state of Texas, USA. The city crosses from west to east the Santa Catarina river, normally the river has no water when is not rainy season, but in rainy season this river becomes dangerous and destructive, and can be deadly. The municipality of Monterrey is limited in the north with the municipalities of San Nicolás de los Garza and General Escobedo; in the south with San Pedro Garza García and Santiago; and in the west east with Guadalupe, Juárez and Cadereyta; in the west with Santa Catarina and García. The city is surrounded by mountains, that is why the city has gained the title of the "city of the mountains".

Tecnológico de Monterrey

Monterrey has the Monterrey Technological and Higher Studies Institute(ITESM) known as well as "Tec de Monterrey", it is a private mexican University. Founded in 1943 by Eugenio Garza Sada, an important businessman graduated from the MIT. This is a high quality university, it has always been vinculated with the mexican business elite, in 2019 was listed as the 15th university in the world with the highest number of graduated being billionaires. It is also considered among the 45 best universities in the world. This university has its headquarters in Monterrey, this brings to the city many young people from inside and outside of the country to study a career with a promising future.

Parque Ecológico La Huasteca

This park is located in the municipality of Santa Catarina, which takes part in the metropolitan area of Monterrey. The park is next to the Santa Catarina river between the Cerro de Chipinque and the Santa Catarina hill, and together are part of the Monterrey Hills National Park. This park attracts a lof of visitors which brings tourism to the zone, the park is usually visted by amateur and professional hikers from around the world. It is also visted by entire families to have a picnic or to just have a nice weekend enjoying the nature of the place.
